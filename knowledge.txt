What you're building — the smart token intelligence pipeline
Forget "check DexScreener and decide." That's a fast bot. What you're describing is a multi-source forensics engine — every token goes through five interrogation layers before a single cent is spent. Here's what each layer does and why it matters:
Layer 1 — Market data (DexScreener). The basic vital signs: price, volume, liquidity, market cap, holder count, token age. A new token with $500k volume in 2 hours is interesting. That same token with zero liquidity is dead. This layer sets the stage; it never decides alone.
Layer 2 — Safety checks (GoPlus + RugCheck + TokenSniffer). This is your first hard wall. Start with RugCheck for the broadest default risk screen, add GoPlus Security for contract-focused signals, Honeypot.is for direct honeypot checks, and TokenSniffer for a familiar second opinion. These three together catch: honeypots (you can buy but never sell), contracts with blacklist/mint functions the deployer can abuse, and cloned scam contracts. Scoring is usually a weighted sum — LP safety, permissions, fee logic, and trade simulation carry most of the weight. Anything that fails here is an immediate reject — no further processing. DEXToolsCryptoLinks
Layer 3 — Liquidity lock and ownership. Even if the contract is clean, legitimate projects lock their liquidity pool tokens using services like Unicrypt or Team Finance. If liquidity is not locked, the deployer can remove it at any time, effectively performing a rug pull. We also check: is ownership renounced? Do the top 10 wallets hold more than 30% of supply? Is the deployer wallet still holding large bags? Kerberus
Layer 4 — Sentiment and social intelligence (ChainGPT). This is where your ChainGPT instinct is correct. ChainGPT's AI Crypto Alerts aggregates news, events and ecosystem signals around tokens from trusted sources, analyzes each for sentiment (bullish/bearish/neutral), and generates a bullishness score based on aggregated signals. Their InfoFI API specifically gives you mindshare scores, KOL (Key Opinion Leader) mentions, and narrative tracking — meaning you can see if a coin is being organically talked about vs. artificially pumped by paid influencers. Chaingpt
Layer 5 — On-chain forensics (BubbleMaps + block explorers). The deepest layer. BubbleMaps shows wallet clustering — if 15 "different" wallets all funded from the same deployer address, that's coordinated manipulation. Block explorer data shows the actual buy/sell ratio in real transactions (not DexScreener's aggregated view) and whether the contract source code is verified.
